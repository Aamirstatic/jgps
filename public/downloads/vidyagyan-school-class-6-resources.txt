------------------------------------------------------------------------
1. VIDYAGYAN RURAL MERITORIOUS ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vidyagyan has very strict eligibility rules. Ensure your child meets them before applying:
* Rural Area Only: Student must be studying in a government or recognized private school located in a rural (village) area of Uttar Pradesh.
* Income Limit: Total annual family income from all sources must not exceed Rs. 1,00,000 (One Lakh Rupees). Income certificate is required.
* Class/Age Limits:
  * For Boys: Student must be studying in Class 5. Age must be between 10 to 11 years.
  * For Girls: Student must be studying in Class 5. Age must be between 10 to 12 years.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
2. VIDYAGYAN TWO-STAGE EXAMINATION PATTERN
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Vidyagyan admission is conducted in two distinct stages:

* STAGE-1: Preliminary Exam (Offline OMR-based)
  * Subjects: Language (Hindi), Mathematics, General Aptitude / IQ.
  * Focus: Assessing basic analytical skills and mental aptitude.
* STAGE-2: Main Exam (Online Computer-based Test)
  * Subjects: Comprehension, Advanced Aptitude, Logical Reasoning.
  * Focus: Interactive questions where student answers on a computer terminal. JGPS provides computer-lab training to help children get comfortable with this.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
3. SUBJECT-WISE PREPARATION HIGHLIGHTS
------------------------------------------------------------------------
* APPTITUDE / IQ:
  [ ] Series completion and logic riddles
  [ ] Visual puzzles and figure matching
  [ ] Analogy tests (Word relationships)
* MATHEMATICS:
  [ ] Basic arithmetic operations (Add, Sub, Mul, Div)
  [ ] Unitary method and real-world calculation word problems
  [ ] Basic shape recognition and measurements
* HINDI:
  [ ] Comprehension passages
  [ ] Basic Hindi grammar (Noun, Verb, Pronoun, Adjectives)
  [ ] Synonyms (Paryayvachi) and Antonyms (Vilom)
