## Section-Wise Preparation Tips

### Mental Ability (40 Questions — Most Important)

This section alone can decide selection. Here is how to prepare:

- Practice 30-50 questions daily from Day 1
- Start with easy questions. Move to hard ones after 2 weeks
- Focus on these high-frequency topics: Series (8 questions), Analogy (8 questions), Pattern Completion (6 questions)
- Time target: 1.5 minutes per question
- Use a timer during practice. Speed matters

**Best practice method:** Solve 10 questions without a timer. Then solve the same 10 again with a 15-minute timer. Compare accuracy. If accuracy drops, slow down.

### Arithmetic (20 Questions)

Math is based on Class 4 and 5 NCERT. Nothing beyond that.

- Revise NCERT Class 4 and 5 math chapters first
- Focus on: Number System, Fractions, Geometry, Measurement
- Practice word problems daily (at least 5)
- Learn multiplication tables up to 20
- Practice mental math (no paper, no calculator)
- Time target: 1.5 minutes per question

**Best practice method:** Solve 20 arithmetic questions every day at 4 PM. Same time daily builds a habit. After 30 days, speed will improve automatically.

### Language (20 Questions)

This is the easiest section for most children. But do not ignore it.

- Read one Hindi and one English passage daily
- Learn 5 new vocabulary words daily (write them in a notebook)
- Practice sentence correction exercises
- Focus on reading speed — your child should read 200 words per minute
- Time target: 1.5 minutes per question

**Best practice method:** Make your child read a story before bed. Ask them to tell you the story in their own words. This builds comprehension naturally.

## 5 Mistakes Parents Must Avoid

**Mistake 1: Not Making a Timetable**
Random studying does not work. Your child needs a fixed schedule. Same time, same subjects, every day. A timetable removes confusion and builds discipline.

**Mistake 2: Ignoring Mental Ability Section**
Parents focus on math because they understand it. But MAT carries 50% of the marks. If your child is weak in MAT, they cannot crack JNVST. Give MAT at least 1 hour daily.

**Mistake 3: Using Old Pattern Books**
The JNVST 2027 pattern changed. Old books have 100 questions. The new exam has 80. Using old books wastes time and builds wrong habits. Always check the publication date.

**Mistake 4: No Mock Test Practice**
Your child may know the answers. But can they solve 80 questions in 2 hours under pressure? Without mock tests, they will panic on exam day. Start mock tests at least 8 weeks before the exam.

**Mistake 5: Too Much Pressure on Child**
Some parents make children study 6-8 hours daily. This backfires. The child gets tired, loses interest, and starts forgetting things. 3-4 hours of focused study beats 8 hours of forced study every time.

## People Also Ask

**Q: Can I crack JNVST in 3 months?**
A: Yes. With a structured plan, 3-4 hours of daily study, and weekly mock tests, your child can crack JNVST in 3 months. Thousands of students do it every year. The key is consistency, not long hours.

**Q: How many hours should my child study for JNVST?**
A: **3 to 4 hours daily** is enough. More than that causes fatigue. Divide the time: 1 hour MAT, 1 hour Arithmetic, 45 minutes Language, and 45 minutes revision. Include one weekly mock test on Sundays.

**Q: Which section is easiest in JNVST?**
A: For most children, the **Language section** is easiest. It has 20 questions on reading comprehension and grammar. With daily reading practice, your child can score 15+ out of 20 easily.

**Q: Is coaching necessary for JNVST preparation?**
A: Coaching helps but is not mandatory. Self-study with NCERT books, previous year papers, and a good preparation plan can work. The key is discipline and daily practice. Coaching adds structure and expert guidance.

**Q: What is the pass mark for JNVST 2027?**
A: There is no fixed pass mark. Selection is based on **district-wise merit**. The cutoff varies by district and category. Generally, scoring **50-60 out of 80** gives a good chance. Rural category cutoff is usually lower.
