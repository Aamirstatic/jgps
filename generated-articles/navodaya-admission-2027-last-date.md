### What Is Navodaya Vidyalaya?

Jawahar Navodaya Vidyalaya (JNV) is a system of schools run by the Government of India. The Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti (NVS) manages all 661 schools across the country. These are residential schools. That means your child lives at the school. Food, books, uniforms, and hostel — everything is free.

The goal is simple. Give talented children from villages and small towns the same quality education that big city kids get. English medium. CBSE board. Modern facilities. And it does not cost you a single rupee.

### Why Parents Dream About JNV

For parents in UP, Bihar, and Uttarakhand, JNV is a dream. Your child gets a boarding school education worth lakhs — for free. The schools have good teachers, science labs, computer rooms, and sports grounds.

Many JNV students later crack IIT, NEET, and other big exams. The environment pushes your child to do better. Classmates are all selected through a tough exam. So the competition inside the school makes your child sharper.

## Who Can Apply — Eligibility Rules 2027

### Age and Class Rules

Your child must meet these rules:

- Must be studying in Class 5 in a recognized school
- Age between 10 to 12 years as on March 31, 2027
- Must be studying in the same district where the JNV is located
- Indian citizen
- No second attempt allowed (can apply only once)

### Rural Quota — 75% Seats for Village Students

This is the best part for rural families. Out of every 80 seats in a JNV, 60 are reserved for children from rural areas. If your child studies in a village school, the chances are much better.

| Feature | Rural Students | Urban Students |
|---|---|---|
| Seats Reserved | 75% (60 out of 80) | 25% (20 out of 80) |
| School Required | Village/Gram Panchayat school | Any recognized school |
| Competition Level | Lower (fewer applicants per seat) | Higher (more applicants per seat) |
| Documents Needed | Rural area certificate from school | Urban area proof |

## How to Apply — Step by Step Guide

**Step 1: Visit the Official Website**
Go to [navodaya.gov.in](https://navodaya.gov.in). Click on "JNVST 2027 Class 6 Admission" link.

**Step 2: Register Your Child**
Fill in your child's name, date of birth, and school details. Make sure the name matches the Class 5 marksheet exactly.

**Step 3: Upload Documents**
You need: passport-size photo, signature, Class 5 marksheet, age proof, and residence certificate. Keep these ready before starting.

**Step 4: Select JNV Preference**
Choose up to 2 JNVs in your district. Pick the nearest one first.

**Step 5: Pay the Fee**
Application is free for most categories. SC/ST/BPL families pay nothing.

**Step 6: Submit and Save**
Download the confirmation page. Take a printout. Keep it safe until the exam.

### Documents Checklist

- Passport-size photograph (recent)
- Child's signature (parent can help)
- Class 5 marksheet or bonafide certificate
- Date of birth certificate
- Residence certificate (for rural quota)
- Caste certificate (if applicable)
- BPL certificate (if applicable)

## What Will Your Child Study — JNVST Syllabus 2027

### Section-wise Topics

**Mental Ability Test (MAT) — 40 Questions**
This section tests how well your child can think and solve problems. Topics include:
- Figure Matching
- Pattern Completion
- Analogy (finding similar things)
- Series completion
- Embedded figures
- Mirror and water images
- Direction sense

This is the most important section. It carries the most marks.

**Arithmetic — 20 Questions**
Math based on Class 4 and 5 NCERT. Topics:
- Number System (place value, rounding off)
- Fractions and Decimals
- Basic Geometry (shapes, angles)
- Measurement (length, weight, time)
- Data handling (tables, bar graphs)
- Word problems

**Language Test — 20 Questions**
Reading comprehension and grammar. Your child will read a passage and answer questions. Also includes:
- Vocabulary
- Sentence correction
- Fill in the blanks

### Exam Pattern

| Section | Questions | Marks | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Mental Ability | 40 | 40 | 60 minutes |
| Arithmetic | 20 | 20 | 30 minutes |
| Language | 20 | 20 | 30 minutes |
| **Total** | **80** | **80** | **2 hours** |

This shows where your child must spend the most time. Mental Ability needs daily practice from Day 1.

## 5 Mistakes Parents Must Avoid

**Mistake 1: Waiting Until the Last Day**
Many parents fill the form on July 30 or 31. The website crashes due to heavy traffic. Your child loses the chance. Fill the form by July 20 at the latest.

**Mistake 2: Wrong Date of Birth**
If the date of birth on the form does not match the birth certificate, the application gets rejected. Double-check every detail before submitting.

**Mistake 3: Not Checking District Eligibility**
Your child must study in a school in the same district as the JNV. If the school is in a different district, the form will be rejected.

**Mistake 4: Ignoring the Rural Quota**
If your child studies in a village school, make sure to apply under the rural quota. The competition is much lower. Many parents miss this and apply in the general category by mistake.

**Mistake 5: No Preparation After Filling Form**
Filling the form is just step one. The real work starts after. Your child needs 3-4 months of focused practice to crack JNVST. Start preparation the same day you submit the form.

## People Also Ask

**Q: What is the last date for Navodaya admission 2027?**
A: The last date to apply for JNVST 2027 Class 6 is **July 31, 2026**. The application form was released on July 6, 2026. Apply at navodaya.gov.in before the deadline.

**Q: Is Navodaya Vidyalaya really free?**
A: Yes, completely free. The government pays for everything — education, hostel, food, books, uniforms, and even medical care. You only need to pay a small monthly mess charge (about Rs 600) for boys in General/OBC category. Girls, SC, and ST students pay nothing.

**Q: Can rural students apply for JNVST 2027?**
A: Yes. In fact, 75% of seats are reserved for rural students. If your child studies in a school located in a village or gram panchayat area, they have a better chance of getting selected.

**Q: What is the age limit for JNVST Class 6?**
A: Your child must be between **10 to 12 years** old as on March 31, 2027. For SC and ST students, there is a 2-year relaxation (up to 14 years).

**Q: How many seats are there in Navodaya Vidyalaya?**
A: Each JNV has **80 seats** for Class 6. There are **661 JNVs** across India. That means about 52,880 total seats for Class 6 admission 2027-28.
