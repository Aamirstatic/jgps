The biggest change is in the number of questions. The exam now has fewer questions but the same time limit. This means your child gets more time per question. But the questions are trickier.

Here is a quick comparison. It will show you the changes in 30 seconds.

## Section-Wise JNVST 2027 Syllabus

### Mental Ability Test (MAT) — 40 Questions

This is the make-or-break section. It carries half the marks (40 out of 80). Your child needs to practice this every single day.

**Topics covered:**

- **Figure Matching** — Find the same figure from options (5-6 questions)
- **Pattern Completion** — Complete the missing part of a pattern (5-6 questions)
- **Analogy** — Find the relationship between two things (6-8 questions)
- **Series** — Find the next number or shape in a sequence (6-8 questions)
- **Embedded Figures** — Find a hidden shape inside a bigger figure (4-5 questions)
- **Mirror and Water Images** — What does an object look like in a mirror? (4-5 questions)
- **Direction Sense** — Left, right, up, down problems (3-4 questions)
- **Paper Folding and Cutting** — What shape comes after folding paper? (3-4 questions)

**Key tip:** MAT is not about memory. It is about practice. The more your child practices, the faster they get. Start with 30 minutes daily.

### Arithmetic — 20 Questions

Math questions are based on Class 4 and 5 NCERT. Nothing beyond that. But speed matters.

**Topics covered:**

- **Number System** — Place value, rounding off, comparing numbers (4-5 questions)
- **Fractions and Decimals** — Addition, subtraction, multiplication of fractions (3-4 questions)
- **Basic Geometry** — Shapes, angles, lines, symmetry (3-4 questions)
- **Measurement** — Length, weight, time, temperature (3-4 questions)
- **Data Handling** — Tables, bar graphs, pictographs (2-3 questions)
- **Word Problems** — Real-life math problems (3-4 questions)

**Key tip:** Practice calculations without a calculator. Your child must be fast with addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division. Speed drills help a lot.

### Language Test — 20 Questions

This section checks reading and understanding. It is in Hindi or English (your child chooses during form filling).

**Topics covered:**

- **Reading Comprehension** — Read a passage, answer questions (8-10 questions)
- **Vocabulary** — Meaning of words, synonyms, antonyms (4-5 questions)
- **Grammar** — Sentence correction, fill in the blanks (4-5 questions)
- **Sentence Formation** — Arrange words to make correct sentences (2-3 questions)

**Key tip:** Make your child read one Hindi and one English story every day. Reading speed improves naturally. This helps in both Language and Arithmetic word problems.

## How to Prepare for New Pattern — 5 Steps

**Step 1: Understand the New Pattern First**
Before buying any book, sit with your child and explain the new pattern. Show them the table above. Let them know what to expect. This removes fear.

**Step 2: Focus More on MAT (Highest Weightage)**
Mental Ability has 40 questions out of 80. That is 50% of the exam. If your child scores well in MAT, they are halfway there. Practice MAT daily for at least 30 minutes.

**Step 3: Use New Pattern Books Only**
Old edition books have 100-question papers. Your child needs 80-question papers that match the new pattern. Check the publication date. Buy books published after July 2026.

**Step 4: Take Weekly Mock Tests**
Every Sunday, give your child a full mock test. 80 questions in 2 hours. No breaks. This builds exam stamina. Track the score each week. You will see improvement.

**Step 5: Time Management Strategy**
Teach your child this order: MAT first (60 min), then Arithmetic (30 min), then Language (30 min). MAT needs fresh brain energy. Do it first. Language is easiest for most children. Save it for last.

## 5 Mistakes Parents Must Avoid

**Mistake 1: Ignoring New Pattern Changes**
Many parents do not know the pattern changed. Their children are still solving 100-question papers. This wastes time and builds wrong habits. Always check the latest prospectus at navodaya.gov.in.

**Mistake 2: Using Old Edition Books**
A 2025 or 2026 book will have the old pattern. The question types, number of questions, and time allocation are all different. Buy only 2027 edition books.

**Mistake 3: Not Practicing Enough MAT**
Parents focus on Arithmetic because it feels familiar. But MAT carries 50% marks. If your child is weak in MAT, they cannot crack JNVST. Give MAT equal or more practice time.

**Mistake 4: Skipping Language Section**
Many rural children are weak in English. So parents skip the Language section. Big mistake. Language has 20 marks. Even 10 extra marks can change the rank. Practice comprehension daily.

**Mistake 5: No Mock Test Practice**
Your child may know all the answers. But can they solve 80 questions in 2 hours? Without mock test practice, they will run out of time in the real exam. Start mock tests at least 2 months before the exam.
